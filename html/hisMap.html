Melnik

At the beginning of the 13th century Melnik was the capital of the estates of despot Alexy Slav, nephew of the brothers Peter, Asen and Kaloyan. In 1207, after the assassination of king Kaloyan, despot Alexius Slav opposed the usurper Boril and withdrew to his lands in the Rhodope-Pirin region as an independent Bulgarian ruler.

Veliko Tarnovo

Veliko Tarnovo was the capital of Bulgaria from 1185, when the Asenevtsi uprising broke out and the Bulgarian state was restored, until 1393, when the city was conquered by the Turks. Some of the Bulgarian kings whose throne was there - the brothers Peter, Assen and Kaloyan, Ivan Assen II, Ivailo, Svetoslav Terter, Michael III Shishman, Ivan Alexander, Ivan Shishman.

Vidin

It is known that king Ivan Alexander in the middle of the 14th century divided the Bulgarian state into the Vidin Kingdom with the capital Vidin and the Tarnovo Kingdom. Ivan Sratsimir, son of Ivan Alexander from his first marriage, was king of the Vidin Kingdom from 1356 to 1396. With a break of 3 years, between 1365 and 1369, the Vidin Kingdom was under Hungarian occupation.
